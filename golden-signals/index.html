The Golden Signals

The 4 "golden signals" of Kubernetes Cost Optimization as defined in a whitepaper released by Google Cloud in June 2023.

  • Resources

    • Workload Rightsizing

    • Demand-based Downscaling

    • Cluster Bin Packing

  • Cloud Discounts

    • Cloud Provider Discount Coverage

These signals help us apply and measure cost optimization for Kubernets clusters.

The 3 signals in the resources group apply to all clusters - be it on-prem or on-cloud.

The cloud discounts naturally only apply to cloud-based managed clusters, where it is very important to pinpoint the instance types and reservation level of our cluster nodes.

Let's explain each signal in a bit more detail.

The Resources Group

Workload Rightsizing

Refers to our ability to allocate the amount of resources that the workloads actually need and adapt resource requests and limits as application requirements change.

Demand based downscaling

Measures the capacity of developers and platform admins to make clusters scale down during off-peak hours.

Cluster bin packing

Refers to our ability to measure and utilize the CPU and memory of each node in the most effective and reliable way through correct Pod placement.

The Cloud Discounts group

Cloud Discount coverage

Refers to leveraging cloud VM instances that offer discounts, such as Spot VMs, as well as the ability of budget owners and FinOps professionals to take advantage of long-term continuous use discounts offered by cloud providers.
